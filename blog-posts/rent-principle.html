Having worked in 3 different countries and rented around 10 different apartments, I have gathered some experience on how to decide if the salary offered by a new job is sufficient or not depending on your living expenses in a particular city and country.

My main learning point: You should always follow the 20 percent rent principle. Read on to learn more.

For most people, rent seems to swallow the biggest chunk of their monthly salaries. Thus, it is always important to find out approximately how much you will have to pay in rent when moving to a new city.

Most people tend to base an approximate amount for rent on either what their peers pay or what they themselves have paid in the past. These are the sole measures used when they decide and conclude whether or not it is a cheap or an expensive place to rent.

This is, however, not an optimal way to decide it as it does not take your actual salary into account.

I believe that a much better way to look at this is by basing it on a percentage taken from your net salary and only allow for a maximum of 20% of your net income per month to go to rent.

If you start doing this you will always have money for savings, investing as well as fun things such as going to restaurants and bars.
